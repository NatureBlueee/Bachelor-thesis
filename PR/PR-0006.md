> "**Role of Research Propositions**
>
> This study is guided by four propositions (P1-P4) derived from the theoretical framework. In qualitative research, propositions serve as **analytical guides** rather than statistical hypotheses to be tested (Gioia et al., 2013). They direct attention to specific phenomena and relationships while remaining open to unexpected findings.
>
> The analysis will evaluate the extent to which interview data **support, refine, challenge, or extend** these propositions (Miles & Huberman, 1994). Findings that contradict initial propositions are particularly valuable as they reveal boundary conditions and theoretical nuances not captured in the original framework."
